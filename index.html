A quick rundown of the topics you'll be enjoying

This section insightfully introduces topics and themes that are motivational, educational and inspirational. He urges them never to give up for there is light at the end of the tunnel despite the challenges they encounter daily

This section of the book contains issues that has to do with politics. He uses poetic language as medium of communication to convey the canker in the African politics. He pleads with politicians, the younger generation and everybody in society to exhibit good attitude that can lead to rapid development. Book Review

If you have ever felt the need to motivate yourself, encourage yourself or perhaps sharpen your self-esteem, there is only ... few books I will recommend in that regard, and ‘Drum Beat’ is definitely on that list. This book does not only bring out the beauty of literature but equally goes a long way to add value to the reader. Thus, in as much as you get entertained reading this amazing book, you will equally get to learn a lot about life, how to tackle the daily struggles it comes with and getting motivated to be more than just ordinary. The choice of words, the imagery and the effective use of simile and metaphor brings the poems to life and makes it extremely relatable. I am convinced that this book; the ‘Drum Beat’ is the birth of a new face of the African poetry. Where everyone can appreciate literature and poems without worry about relating and understanding core themes and subject matter of the poems. I have read and appreciated a lot of African poetry and I must state without an iota of doubt that this book counts among the best. It follows Chinua Achebe’s admonishment to always use literature to touch on some key important ills or social canker in the continent of Africa. You can decide to ignore the literary devices, you can purposely overlook the play with words among others but one thing you cannot ignore is the issues that the various poems seek to address. Initially, I thought I was reading for the fun of it but I was caught thinking about the essential themes raised by the poet and it wasn’t long before I took them to work. It was amazing how quickly the work had an effect on my life but I realized that was how amazing the book is. AB is an amazing personality with a blessed hand that makes the pen write for generational readers and leaders. I have read every page of this book and without reservation, I will endorse this book for every reader especially those who seek to find their path in life and encourage themselves while they are at it.

Benard Makafui Mordedzi
Author; The Devil In Me

It is heartwarming to observe that the blooming of new writers has gradually taken shape. These include works such as ... Kwame Wright, Philip Boakye Dua Oyinka popularly known as Nana Asaase and Abraham A. Anab. His concise but incisive short collection of poems entitled “The Drum Beat” symbolizes an appeal to the youth to take action for a better future. The Drum Beat is set in simple but flowery language which is intrinsically woven to drive home virtues such as diligence, perseverance, belief in God and the can-do spirit. Though the collection is divided into four broad areas of educational/motivational, politics, faith and religion as well as love and life, each piece seems to have pieces of these meshed into one another to have the required effect. The Drum Beat symbolizes talents, sound, light and a call for action. In terms of style, Anab’s collection makes use of allusion, symbolism and extended metaphors and similes to gain the needed effect. He equally creates parallels among the poems which echo the same idea in a different and subtle manner. The poems come in various lengths including Pleasure which is a two-line poem. The Drum Beat is an invitation for Ghanaians of all walk of life, including the youth to take advantage of their talents to contribute to national development. It recognizes the need to convert initial challenges and failures to stepping stones on the path to progress.

Mr. Samuel Y. Amebley
University of Ghana School of Law
